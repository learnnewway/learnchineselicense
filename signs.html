Visual Recognition

Exam Category: Signals & Signs

Police Hand Signals (Override all electronic lights)

Stop Signal Left arm raised straight up, palm facing forward. ALL traffic must stop.

Straight Ahead Left arm raised sideways, right arm sweeps across chest. Proceed straight.

Left Turn Right arm points to the right, left arm sweeps forward. Left-turning traffic may proceed.

Pull Over Left arm raised straight up, right arm points down and sweeps horizontally. Pull over to the right and stop.

Memorize the arm angles. The left arm usually indicates the target traffic, the right arm indicates the action.

Prohibition Signs

Shape/Color: White background, red circle, black symbol (often with a red slash).

Logic: "Do Not Do This."

  • No Entry (Solid red circle with white bar)
  • No Parking (Red circle, blue background, red slash)
  • No Honking (Trumpet with red slash)

Warning Signs

Shape/Color: Yellow triangle, black border, black symbol.

Logic: "Hazard Ahead, Slow Down."

  • Sharp Curve Ahead
  • Pedestrian Crossing Ahead (Different from the actual crossing zone)
  • Village/Town Ahead

Mandatory Signs

Shape/Color: Blue circle, white symbol.

Logic: "You MUST Do This."

  • Go Straight Only
  • Turn Left Only
  • Honk Horn (Usually required on blind mountain curves)

Road Surface Markings

Yellow Lines

Separate traffic flowing in opposite directions. A solid yellow line means NO crossing or U-turns. A broken yellow line means you can cross if safe.

White Lines

Separate traffic flowing in the same direction. Solid white means no lane changing. Broken white means lane changing is permitted.

Diamond Markings

White diamond shapes painted on the lane indicate a pedestrian crosswalk is approaching. You MUST decelerate.
