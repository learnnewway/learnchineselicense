First Aid Protocols

Arterial Bleeding: Blood is bright red and spurts. Apply pressure closer to the heart (above the wound).

Venous Bleeding: Blood is dark red and flows continuously. Apply pressure away from the heart (below the wound).

Fractures: NEVER attempt to reset a protruding bone. Keep the casualty immobilized and splint in place.

Burns: Spray with cold water. Do NOT pop blisters. Do NOT peel off clothing stuck to the skin.

Mechanical & Environmental

ABS Braking: In an emergency, press the brake pedal down firmly and hold it. Never pump ABS brakes. ABS allows you to steer while braking.

Tire Blowout: Hold the steering wheel firmly with both hands, release the accelerator, and brake gently. NEVER slam the brakes.

Fog Protocol: Turn on fog lights and hazard lights. NEVER use high beams as the light reflects off the fog and blinds you.

Aquaplaning: Release the accelerator immediately. Keep the steering wheel straight. Do not brake hard.
